About

Pinagsama (Tagalog: [pinagsama] lit. "union") is an administrative division of Metro Manila in the Philippines. It is located in the western part of Taguig City. The McKinley Hill development is adjacent to the area, a thriving community of mixed residential and commercial development along Ring Road 5 (C5), and Bonifacio Global City is located in the Arca South mixed-use development. It is bordered by barangay Fort Bonifacio to the north, Central Signal Village to the southeast, North Signal Village to the east, Ussan to the northeast, and West Bicutan to the west and southwest. Pinagsama Creek, a tributary of the Taguig River, runs through the northern half of Pinagsama and Ussan.

History

Barangay Pinagsama was separated from Barangay West Bicutan by Taguig City Ordinance No. 2. His 67 series dated September 15, 2008. This name was conceived due to the presence of neighboring territorial villages of Wildcat Village, 16th ISU Village, ISG Central Village, G2 Village, Upper West Village, Panam Village, Parar Village and Pinagsama Village Phase. Phase 2, AFP EP Village Phase 1 and Phase 2, Heritage Park, Inner Fort Bonifacio Subdivision, McKinley Hill. An official referendum was held in Barangay Western Bicutan Island on December 12, 2008, and an overwhelming majority supported the creation of a new barangay.

International Gateway

Barangay Pinagsama is renowned for housing notable landmarks such as the Venice Grand Canal Mall, as well as the Embassies of the United Kingdom, South Korea, the State of Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates. Meanwhile, McKinley Hill serves as the location for an esteemed international school and college, earning its reputation as the "International Gateway of Taguig City."

Location

Pinagsama is one of the 28 barangays of Taguig City in Metro Manila, Philippines. Barangay Pinagsama is under 2nd Legislative district of Taguig. In 2008, Western Bicutan was divided into three barangays, Fort Bonifacio, Pinagsama and Western Bicutan (originating barangay).
